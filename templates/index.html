Chapter One. Radio

1 Peter 5:8-9: Warns to be sober and vigilant because the devil prowls around like a roaring lion, seeking someone to devour, and instructs believers to resist him.

The nightmare starts the same way the others always do, with the false hope that this time, somehow, things might be different.

Jayce finds himself back within the old house’s enclosure, its abandoned walls disguised as plain brick. The image splits his mind in two, down to the root. A part of him hold onto what's in front of him, without sparing a single thought to the premonition The air is damp, In the dreams, the details always shift; this time, the stone is covered in a thousand closed eyes.

“Okay, wrap it up!” an agent calls from across the yard.

The midnight air bites at his neck, and he feels youth settle into his twenty-seven-year-old body. Back when the only signs of age were the smile lines at his mouth, when the world seemed unprepared for him—when his skills felt sufficient, and he believed himself smart.

Tonight, that belief has teeth. The aberration is neutralized. The case is closed. His shoulders, for once, are light.

It’s time to go home.

“Come on, Jayce. This is probably going to be my only chance to get him alone.” Amaranthine’s voice rings out, and he turns to look at her, the sight feels like an ache on his chest.

He fears he may be starting to forget her face, but the night terrors manage to preserve every detail, from the baby fat that hung in her cheeks to the mischievous smirk she always wore. His too-young, too-ingenuous protégée, the one he was meant to look after.

Even if it’s only a dream, he feels his own mind regressing.

He can’t remember what made him agree to let her go—perhaps the reassurance that professor Heimerdinger would be inside to receive her, or maybe the excitement that lit her eyes at what might have been her only chance to impress him with her investigation.

Either way, he wants to claw at his own throat when he hears his voice mutter the response that would haunt him for years to come. He hears it as if from outside himself, wants to grab that earlier version of his body and shake it, to scream at him to take it back, to say anything else. “All right. Just be quick.”

She smiles at him, and he releases the warmth of her hand.

He watches her slip away, her blonde head bobbing as she runs toward the house, her frame swallowed by the darkness inside.

Jayce waits twenty, maybe twenty-five minutes, too deep in thought to notice the cold creeping deeper into the air, until the car radio turns on—or maybe it never turned off. Maybe the signs were always there. The thought would haunt him forever.

The device emits a strange noise, broadcasting at an unknown frequency, too deep to decipher, even for someone who reads signals like a second language.

He sits motionless as reality settles in.

The walls snap their eyes open. Their accusatory gaze fixes on him, and for the first time, Jayce is truly seen. He cannot comprehend how small he must appear before the vast, lurking evil.

He tries to run back inside, but the door is sealed shut. What happens next comes only in fragments, memories his body recalls as spasms. He sees himself slamming against the door until it gives, forcing his way in, shouting for help—too late to realize everything has gone silent. Blackish blood streaks the halls. The metallic scent mixes with something sour, a stench that feels like fear made physical.

He finds the first piece of Amaranthine just inside the hall—her foot. It is small, ghostly white from drained circulation. The horrifying sight tugs his heart.

He follows the trail deeper into the house. A hand. An arm.

In a darkened room, he finds her head. Blonde hair clings at the nape where the cut is clean.

Her flesh is still tender when he gathers what remains of her into his arms, but the warmth is gone. Her dead eyes stare past him, blood and water mingling on her skin. Even now, he can read the terror that must have filled her final minutes.

The rest of her forms a deliberate path, leading him back to Heimerdinger’s decimated corpse.

Outside, unaware, every investigator who made up the backup will later resurface from the lake as a corpse.

Then the car radio blares to life, ten times louder than before. This time he understands the message it carries: frantic laughter.

His eyes snap open just as the scream is about to tear from his throat.

12 Deaths Attributed to Gas Leak in Buffalo, New York

By Seraphine Baker

A week has passed since the incident that shook the entire city: the sudden death of 13 citizens inside a warehouse located directly across from 2147 Glenmoor Avenue.

Following an intense wave of media coverage, the local police department’s initial claim, suggesting the deaths might be connected to the recent disappearances near Lake Erie, has now shifted. What first appeared to be a bigger investigation washing their hands from earlier implications. Public statements regarding the incident gradually became more restrained, and responsibility for further communication seemed to move into the hands of a higher authority.
